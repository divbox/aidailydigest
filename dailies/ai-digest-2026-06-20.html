Perplexity Brain: Self-Improving Memory for Agents
Perplexity Brain builds a persistent context graph across tasks, projects, decisions, and files — so agents start every session with relevant prior knowledge instead of a blank slate. Every memory is tied to its original source and the graph updates continuously over time. The practical payoff is better task accuracy and lower costs through retrieval reuse rather than re-deriving everything from scratch.

Safety & Policy
MosaicLeaks: Deep Research Agents Leak Private Data 34% of the Time
Research agents that combine private documents with open web retrieval will leak sensitive information in roughly a third of queries. The fix, PA-DR, drops that rate to under 10% by training agents to construct safe queries via RL rewards — not user prompts, which are easy to bypass. The framing matters: this is a training problem, not a prompting problem.
