Demis Hassabis calls for a US-led global AI standards body
DeepMind's CEO published a manifesto proposing a FINRA-style independent standards body — majority independent board, industry/government/open-source seats — to review frontier models on cyber and bio-risk benchmarks up to 30 days before release. Compliance starts voluntary; Hassabis wants it operational "before year-end" and says other lab heads broadly agree on the direction. Manifestos are cheap; getting competing labs to actually submit models 30 days early is the part worth watching.

PrismML's Bonsai 27B runs a 27B model on an iPhone
A 1-bit/1.58-bit quantized version of Qwen3.6-27B, compressed from 54GB down to 3.9GB, hitting 11 tokens/sec on an iPhone 17 Pro while staying multimodal and agentic-capable. Apple is reportedly already in early talks with PrismML about the compression technique — which is the more interesting story than the model checkpoint itself.
