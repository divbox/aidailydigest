Models & Research
Kimi K3 2.8T-A50B: the largest open model ever released
Latent Space

Moonshot released Kimi K3 as "Open Frontier Intelligence" — 2.8T total parameters, a 1M-token context window, native multimodal input, and two new architecture pieces: Kimi Delta Attention (KDA), claimed to deliver up to 6.3x faster decoding at million-token context, and Attention Residuals (AttnRes), good for roughly 25% higher training efficiency at under 2% extra compute cost. Open weights are promised by July 27; until then the model is live on Kimi.com, Kimi Work, Kimi Code, and the API. Community teardowns of the technical blog also surfaced Stable LatentMoE (16 of 896 experts activated, under 2% activation ratio), per-head Muon, quantile load balancing, and a new SiTU activation function.

Independent evals back up the frontier framing. Artificial Analysis scored K3 at 57 on its Intelligence Index — comparable to Opus 4.8 and GPT-5.5, still behind Fable 5 and GPT-5.6 Sol — while noting a 21% drop in output tokens consumed relative to K2.6 for a 13-point index gain. On Arena, K3 jumped from #18 to #1 in Frontend Code Arena with a 76% pairwise win rate (vs. 63% for Fable 5), and landed at #9 in the general Text Arena, up from #38. The one clear regression: hallucination rate on AA-Omniscience worsened to 51% from 39% even as raw accuracy improved.

Pricing lands at $3/1M input, $15/1M output tokens (90% off for cached input), which multiple posters compared directly to Sonnet 5 — Artificial Analysis put average cost per Intelligence Index task at $0.94, undercutting GPT-5.6 Sol ($1.04) and Opus 4.8 ($1.80). But "open weights" doesn't mean cheap to self-host: Moonshot's own deployment guidance calls for 64+ accelerators, and early API throughput measured around 26-28 tok/s, slower than Opus — likely because speculative decoding wasn't yet enabled.

Reaction split along predictable lines. Supporters called it a "DeepSeek moment" and evidence the US-China capability gap has narrowed from months to weeks; skeptics pointed to the hallucination regression, a disputed ProgramBench scoring methodology that may inflate partial-credit results, and the fact that benchmark parity doesn't equal product/UX parity — a gap Moonshot itself acknowledged relative to Fable 5 and GPT-5.6 Sol. vLLM confirmed Moonshot upstreamed a KDA prefix-caching implementation for day-0 support, a genuine open-source infrastructure contribution rather than just a benchmark flex.

Three things to watch: whether the weights actually ship on July 27, what third-party serving stacks get for real-world throughput, and how K3 holds up on hidden or uncontaminated evals like LiveBench rather than the public benchmark set it just topped.
